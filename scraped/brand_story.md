## Case Study Summaries

### Service Spring Corporation (SSC)
- **Customer**: McAlear Group DBA Service Spring Corporation
- **Industry**: Manufacturing (overhead door industry)
- **Location**: Maumee, Ohio, USA
- **Challenge**: Outdated legacy network did not support CIP/IO and PTP protocols. PLCs lost time synchronization, servos and frequency drives went offline causing productivity loss and material waste.
- **Solution**: Allied Telesis x250-18XS core switches (stacked for HA), IE340-12GT industrial Layer 3 switches with PTP/CIP/IO support, x230-28GT edge switches. Dedicated industrial network physically separated from business network.
- **Results**: Far less downtime, deterministic communication achieved, planning to expand to second manufacturing plant.
- **Key Quote**: "The most important feature for us was support for PTP. Other vendors had the support, but the cost was extreme."
- **Source**: https://www.alliedtelesis.com/us/en/success-stories/service-spring-delivers-precision-prevents-costly-downtime

### Nicholas County Schools (West Virginia)
- **Customer**: Nicholas County Schools
- **Partner**: Sinclair Digital
- **Industry**: Education (K-12)
- **Location**: West Virginia, USA
- **Challenge**: 2016 floods destroyed two schools. Needed state-of-the-art 110,000 sq ft K-12 complex with future-proof technology on a rural district budget.
- **Solution**: Sinclair Digital AGILE-CORE Distributed Edge Architecture with Allied Telesis x950 core switches, IE220 edge-hardened industrial switches, wireless APs, Vista Manager. StrandWise hybrid cables distributing fiber + fault managed DC power up to 600 meters.
- **Results**: 50-year network, $1M+ savings vs conventional design, up to 30% energy savings, eliminated two hardware refresh cycles, first-of-kind architecture for K-12.
- **Key Data Points**: 40 Edge IDFs, 58 wireless APs, 102 cameras, 53 multimedia displays, 40 VoIP phones, 78 panic buttons, 252 PA display/clocks, 85 data jacks, 12 access control boards.
- **Source**: https://www.alliedtelesis.com/us/en/success-stories/west-virginia-school-saves-millions-50-year-network

### Caritas-Klinik Dominikus (Berlin Hospital)
- **Customer**: Caritas-Klinik Dominikus
- **Partner**: TELBA GmbH (Platinum Partner)
- **Industry**: Healthcare
- **Location**: Berlin, Germany
- **Challenge**: Needed free patient Wi-Fi plus full hospital wireless/wired integration. Network had to support diagnostic imaging, patient data systems, and meet healthcare uptime requirements. Implementation had to happen during hospital operation.
- **Solution**: Integrated wired + wireless network with TQ5403 hybrid APs (world-first simultaneous single-channel and multi-channel), AMF for automated management, AWC for wireless optimization, Vista Manager EX for unified management.
- **Results**: "One of the most modern and future-oriented solutions in Germany." Nonstop Wi-Fi, improved diagnostic imaging access, simplified administration, reduced IT overhead.
- **Source**: https://www.alliedtelesis.com/us/en/success-stories/hybrid-wi-fi-solution-provides-seamless-online-access-major-city-hospital

## Key Technology Platforms

### Autonomous Management Framework Plus (AMF Plus)
Scalable network management platform supporting Allied Telesis switching, firewall, and wireless products plus wide range of third-party devices. Features: automated firmware upgrades, backup, recovery, zero-touch provisioning.
Source: Healthcare guide, success stories

### Vista Manager EX
Unified single-pane-of-glass network management for monitoring, managing, and automating wired, wireless, and SD-WAN networks. Creates complete topology maps. Includes health monitoring dashboard.
Source: Multiple pages

### Autonomous Wave Control (AWC)
AI-powered wireless optimization that automatically analyzes traffic and optimizes coverage for maximum performance and minimal interference. Reduces deployment and operating costs.
Source: Healthcare guide, success stories

### AWC Channel Blanket (AWC-CB)
Single-channel Wi-Fi solution enabling seamless roaming for devices on the move. All APs on same blanket operate on same channel. Supports device location tracking.
Source: Healthcare guide

### VCStack (Virtual Chassis Stacking)
Connects multiple switches as single virtual switch. Provides device and path resiliency. Can be spread over long distances with fiber. Add/remove stack members without downtime.
Source: Non-stop networks page, healthcare guide

### EPSRing (Ethernet Protection Switched Ring)
Ring-based network topology for high availability and redundancy. Recovery time as low as 50ms. Ideal for distributed network environments.
Source: Healthcare guide, Rio de Janeiro case study reference

### Self-Defending Network / AMF Security
Automated edge security using AMF-Sec Controller with Isolation Adapter technology. OpenFlow v1.3 compatible. Works for both wired and wireless. Automatically quarantines suspect devices without endpoint agents.
Source: Healthcare guide, awards (multiple cybersecurity awards)

### SD-WAN
Centralized management of branch office connections. Automatically optimizes and load-balances application delivery. Integrated with Application-Aware Firewalls.
Source: Healthcare guide

### OneConnect
Newly launched cloud-based platform designed to unify and simplify network management across wired, wireless, and WAN infrastructures.
Source: Company page news release

## Non-Stop Networks - Detailed Architecture

### Four Layers of Resiliency
1. **Hardware Resiliency**: N+1 redundant power supplies, active-active resilient switching, In-Service Software Upgrade (ISSU)
2. **Connection Resiliency**: VCStack for single-device management of stack, add/remove members without downtime, long-distance stacking for disaster recovery
3. **Network Resiliency**: Bi-directional Forwarding Detection (BFD) for consistent fast failover; works with Layer 2 (STP, RSTP, EPSR) and Layer 3 (OSPF, BGP) protocols
4. **User Access Resiliency**: No Compromise Wi-Fi with seamless roaming, self-tuning wireless, simplified installation
